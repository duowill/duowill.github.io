U.S. County Income Growth Analysis



Role: Sole Contributor

Tools: Excel, Word

Concepts: Data analysis, hypothesis testing, regression analysis, data visualization, report writing

Description:
• Term project for Econometrics 461: Economic Data Analysis course at Texas A&M University
• Statistical analysis of U.S. county-level income growth rate and population growth rate data (1970-2020) for the potential existence of a causal relationship

Responsibilities:
• Conducted multi-region regression and correlation analyses on 3075 rows of U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis real GDP data using Excel to identify potential socioeconomic predictors of income growth
• Built and tested hypotheses across 8 geographic subgroups using t-tests, chi-squared, ANOVA, and F-statistics to assess variance and mean differences in per capita income growth rate related to population growth rate
• Visualized income and population growth trends through frequency distributions, tables, box plots, and scatter plots, delivering statistical insights to reject initial hypothesis concerning region-specific trends
