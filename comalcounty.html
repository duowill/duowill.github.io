Comal County Liquor Sales Forecasting



Role: Team Lead

Tools: R, SQL, Excel, Word

Concepts: Data cleaning, time series analysis, multivariate modeling, trend forecasting, cross-functional collaboration

Description:
• Term project for Econometrics 475: Economic Forecasting course at Texas A&M University
• Tasked with developing forecasting models of Texas county-level beer, wine, and liquor sales for an assigned county using "liquor by the drink" sales data

Responsibilities:
• Led a team of 4 to analyze historical liquor sales data for Comal County, Texas and build a forecasting model to predict future sales trends 12 months ahead
• Queried and extracted 1648 rows of data to forecast monthly alcohol sales using time series models (ETS, ARIMA) in R; identified ETS(M,A,A) as optimal based on residual diagnostics and predictive performance
• Built a multivariate VAR model incorporating employment and total sales data to capture economic drivers of local consumer behavior; confirmed model stability and statistical significance across 193 observations
• Performed seasonal, trend, and cyclical decomposition to uncover purchasing trends, improve forecast accuracy, account for economic anomalies, and offer insights for retail planning and inventory management
